# Competitor: Prosper202

- **URL:** https://prosper202.com
- **Category:** direct
- **Core Value Prop:** Free, self-hosted affiliate tracking solution offering 100% data ownership and zero monthly subscription costs. Designed for experienced affiliates and developers who want full control over their tracking infrastructure.
- **Key Features:**
  1. Completely free to download and use — no subscription fees
  2. Self-hosted on your own server — 100% data ownership and control
  3. Real-time click and conversion tracking with Sub-ID parameters
  4. S2S postback tracking and referrer tracking
  5. Split testing with automatic traffic routing to winning offers
  6. Geo-targeting and device-based traffic rules
  7. "BlazerCache" technology for faster redirects
  8. "Sentinel Traffic Quality Enforcer" (T.Q.E.) fraud prevention
  9. Profit/loss metrics and multi-dimensional reporting (keywords, geo, device, browser, ISP)
  10. Full server-side deployment — no third-party data sharing
- **Known Weaknesses:**
  - Requires significant technical expertise to install and maintain
  - Dated UI/UX — interface looks and feels like early-2010s software
  - No free technical support — relies on community forums and paid support plans
  - "Too many connections" database errors under load
  - Active development appears limited — slow feature updates
  - No cloud/SaaS option — must manage your own VPS
- **Pricing Model:** Free (open-source/self-hosted). Users pay only for their own server costs (VPS ~$5–50/mo). Paid support plans available separately.
- **User Traction:** Legacy tool with declining user base. Estimated 2K–5K remaining active installations. Still used by a niche of experienced affiliates who prefer self-hosting and data sovereignty. Was popular in 2012–2018 era.
- **Unique Differentiator:** Zero recurring cost with full data ownership — the only major tracker with no subscription, no event limits, and complete server-side control.
- **What users complain about:**
  - Outdated, clunky interface compared to modern SaaS trackers
  - Complex installation and server maintenance requirements
  - Database connection errors under high traffic loads
  - No active development — features are stagnant
  - No free support — must troubleshoot via forums or pay for support
  - Not suitable for beginners — steep technical barrier
